Africa - The Cradle of Civilization

Overview

Africa is the second-largest continent in the world and is known for its incredible cultural diversity, ancient civilizations, and breathtaking natural landscapes. It is often called the cradle of humanity because some of the earliest human fossils were discovered here.

From the vast Sahara Desert and the Nile River to tropical rainforests and spectacular wildlife reserves, Africa offers unforgettable travel experiences. The continent is rich in traditions, music, art, and history that continue to influence the world today.

Key Facts

  • Area: Approximately 30.37 million km²
  • Population: Over 1.4 billion people
  • Countries: 54 recognized countries
  • Major Languages: Arabic, Swahili, English, French, Portuguese, and over 2,000 indigenous languages

Popular Countries to Visit

Discover some of the most remarkable and culturally rich destinations across Africa:

Kenya

Capital: Nairobi

Kenya is world-famous for its wildlife safaris and diverse landscapes, ranging from savannas to mountains and coastal beaches along the Indian Ocean.

Witness the Great Migration in the Masai Mara, see elephants beneath Mount Kilimanjaro in Amboseli National Park, and relax on the beautiful beaches of Mombasa.

Egypt

Capital: Cairo

Egypt is one of the world’s oldest civilizations, famous for its ancient monuments and powerful pharaohs. It has played a vital role in human history for thousands of years.

Marvel at the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx, cruise along the Nile River, explore Luxor’s temples and the Valley of the Kings, and discover treasures in the Egyptian Museum.
